How the first page gets shaped

Rarity is not just age. It is significance, provenance, and restraint.

This page defines the approved first-page logic in a simple visual form. The goal is to keep historically significant classics prominent without overlooking genuine modern special editions. Intrinsic collector importance comes first; rare specifications and live-market context refine the order rather than replacing it.

What rises to the top

Cars earn an intrinsic rarity score from model significance and factory specification. A portfolio selector then keeps the first page balanced, intentional, and varied.

Top priority Foundational classic icons first, followed by halo models, homologation cars, and rare factory specification.
Secondary lift PTS, WLS, WTL, Sonderwunsch, Exclusive Manufaktur, low mileage, matching numbers, and provenance.
Portfolio guardrails Top 10: 5 classics, 3 modern specials, 2 open. Every variant shares the same repetition limit.

Score logic

We start with the strongest collector signals, then add supporting factory and provenance details. Scarcity is a tie-breaker only: a lower-significance modern car cannot jump ahead of a stronger classic merely because fewer examples are listed.

  • +76
    Historic classic icon
    A narrow list of foundational archetypes: 356 Speedster, Carrera RS, early 911 S, 930 3.0, rare factory Speedsters, and equivalent air-cooled icons.
  • +70
    Hypercar / halo model
    959, Carrera GT, and 918 remain headline cars, immediately below foundational classic icons.
  • +36
    Homologation special
    Cars built for a very specific purpose, not just general appeal.
  • +32
    Classic significance
    A lineage signal for important classic generations. Age alone never creates historic-icon status.
  • +28
    Paint to sample
    A documented factory color materially strengthens an already significant specification.
  • +18
    GT / Turbo heritage
    Keeps serious performance variants in front of ordinary trims.
  • +16
    WLS factory power upgrade
    Recognizes documented Werksleistungssteigerung cars as distinct factory specifications.
  • +14
    WTL, Sonderwunsch, limited editions, racing history
    Adds weight for factory Turbo-look bodies, special wishes, motorsport links, and limited production.
  • +10
    Narrow-body Speedster / matching numbers / bucket seats
    Important supporting specification and provenance signals, but not enough on their own.
  • +8/+6
    Sunroof delete / Exclusive Manufaktur
    Only an explicit sunroof-delete claim scores; simply mentioning a sunroof does not.

How the ranking behaves

The system is opinionated on purpose. It prefers a few exceptional cars over many similar ones.

1
Start with model significance Rare factory cars and important air-cooled models begin ahead of ordinary listings.
2
Compare collector evidence Variant certainty, provenance signals, and usable photography are compared before live-market scarcity.
3
Build a collector portfolio The Top 10 guarantees five classics and three genuine modern specials, with two open positions for the strongest remaining cars.
4
Apply equal diversity rules No canonical variant gets more than two places in the Top 10 or five in the Top 50. Speedsters compete under the same rule as every other variant.

What gets extra credit

Some details are especially powerful when they appear on the right generation of car.

Air-cooled classics

Classic significance gets a bonus. If they also have PTS, homologation, or Turbo heritage, the score compounds.

WLS and WTL

Factory power upgrades and Turbo-look bodies are identified as distinct collector specifications, including transition-year cars.

Modern special editions

918 Spyder, GT/RS, modern Speedster, Sport Classic, Sonderwunsch, and other explicit factory specials retain guaranteed visibility.

Landing-page scarcity: 0-15

Recognized modern variants only. One active car earns 15; the most supplied variant earns 0. Between them: round[15 * (1 - ln(supply) / ln(max modern supply))]. Duplicate VINs count once.

Examples

A 356 Speedster or Carrera RS can stay ahead of a 918 because foundational historical importance is the first comparison. A 918 Spyder or GT3 RS remains prominent because it qualifies as a genuine modern special, not simply because it is newer or scarce.

Usually ranks higher Usually ranks lower
356 Speedster, Carrera RS, early 911 S, 930 3.0 Ordinary modern 911s with no special specification
Air-cooled WLS, WTL, Speedster, or PTS cars with honest provenance Late-model cars with only generic low-mileage appeal
959, Carrera GT, 918 Spyder, GT/RS, modern Speedster, or documented Sonderwunsch builds Common modern trims made scarce only by current listing supply

The page is deliberately classic-prominent and special-car-first: five classic positions, three modern-special positions, and two open positions in the Top 10. Across the Top 50, at least twenty are classics and ten are modern specials.
